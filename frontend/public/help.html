Quick Answers

How do I sign up?

Click the "Login" button at the top-right and connect with your Discord account. Your account is created automatically.

What happens to my data?

We only store your Discord username and avatar. We never access your passwords or personal messages. Read our Privacy Policy for details.

How do I report a scam or problem?

Use the Contact form to report issues, or reach out in our Discord community.

Is TiltCheck free?

Yes! All core features are free. Most tools and trust scoring are available to everyone. Some premium features may be added later.

Can I delete my account?

Yes. Contact support with your request and we'll delete your data within 30 days.

Do you have a mobile app?

We offer a PWA (Progressive Web App) that works on mobile. Click "Install PWA" on the dashboard or use your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option.

Glossary

Tilt

A mental state where a player makes impulsive decisions due to frustration, anger, or emotional distress after losses. TiltCheck monitors patterns to detect when players may be experiencing tilt.

Degen (Degenerate)

Community slang for a player who takes high-risk bets or plays frequently. While sometimes used humorously, TiltCheck uses "Player" or "Community member" in formal contexts for inclusivity.

Degen Trust Score / Player Trust Score

A score (0-100) representing a player's reliability and behavior patterns within the community. Based on tilt frequency, bonus abuse patterns, and community feedback.

Casino Trust Score

A score (0-100) evaluating a casino's fairness, payout reliability, bonus transparency, and compliance. Updated every 6 hours based on community reports and automated monitoring.

Non-Custodial Tipping

A tipping system where funds move directly from sender to recipient without TiltCheck holding or storing any cryptocurrency. We charge only a flat fee (~0.0007 SOL) per transaction.

Scam Flags

Automated indicators that warn users about potentially fraudulent casinos, phishing links, or suspicious promotions. Triggered by domain age, community reports, and pattern recognition.

SusLink

TiltCheck's AI-powered link scanner that detects scam sites, redirects, and impersonation attempts before you click. Integrates with Discord to automatically flag suspicious URLs.

JustTheTip

Our non-custodial tipping tool for sending cryptocurrency tips, airdrops, and swaps directly in Discord with a flat fee model.

CollectClock

Daily bonus tracker that monitors casino promotions, detects bonus nerfs (reductions), and predicts optimal collection times.

FreeSpinScan

Promo submission system with automatic classification, moderator approval queue, and bonus prediction engine for casino promotions.

Bonus Nerf

When a casino reduces the value or availability of a bonus or promotion. TiltCheck tracks these changes to keep players informed.

RTP (Return to Player)

The percentage of wagered money a game returns to players over time. Higher RTP means better odds for players.

Provably Fair

A cryptographic method that allows players to verify that game outcomes are truly random and not manipulated by the casino.

Rage-Quit Mode

An extreme tilt state where a player is likely to make very poor decisions or stop playing entirely out of frustration.

PWA (Progressive Web App)

A web application that can be installed on your device and works offline, providing a native app-like experience.

Legal Rights Alert

Warnings about casinos that trigger legal compliance concerns, such as payout delays over 7 days, hidden bonus terms, or license violations.

Legal Rights & Player Protection

Understanding your rights when dealing with online casinos can help you avoid disputes and protect your funds.

When should I be concerned about a casino?

Watch for these red flags: Payout delays over 7 days without explanation, hidden bonus terms that appear only after you've deposited, license violations or expired licenses, sudden account restrictions after you win, or unresponsive customer support when you have a withdrawal pending.

What triggers a TiltCheck Legal Rights Alert?

We issue alerts when we detect: withdrawal delays exceeding 7 business days, multiple user reports of frozen accounts, changes to bonus terms mid-campaign, license suspension or revocation, pattern of confiscating winnings citing vague "terms violations," or failure to honor advertised RTP (Return to Player) rates.

What are my rights if a casino refuses to pay out?

If a licensed casino refuses withdrawal: (1) Document everything (screenshots, emails, transaction IDs). (2) File a complaint with their licensing authority (Cura√ßao Gaming Control Board, Malta Gaming Authority, UK Gambling Commission, etc.). (3) Report to player advocacy sites like AskGamblers or Casino Guru. (4) Report to TiltCheck to warn our community. Note: Unlicensed casinos offer no formal recourse‚Äîavoid them entirely.

Can I dispute hidden bonus terms?

Yes, if terms were changed after you accepted the bonus or were not clearly displayed. Licensed casinos must disclose wagering requirements, maximum bet limits, and game restrictions upfront. If they added restrictions later, file a complaint with their regulator citing the date/time you accepted the bonus.

What should I do if I suspect a casino is rigged?

Check if the casino uses Provably Fair technology (lets you verify each game outcome). For traditional RNG games, verify they're certified by testing labs like eCOGRA, iTech Labs, or GLI. If you suspect manipulation, request your game history and compare your loss rate to the advertised RTP. Large deviations over 1,000+ spins may indicate issues‚Äîreport to the licensing authority.

How do I report a scam casino to TiltCheck?

Use our Contact Form or join our Discord. Provide: casino name and URL, description of the issue, screenshots or evidence, dates of deposit/withdrawal attempts, and any communication with their support. We'll investigate and update our trust scores accordingly.

Does TiltCheck provide legal advice?

No. TiltCheck provides community-driven trust scores and scam warnings, but we are not lawyers. For legal disputes involving significant amounts, consult a gambling attorney or your jurisdiction's gaming regulatory body. We help you avoid problems‚Äînot resolve legal battles after they happen.
