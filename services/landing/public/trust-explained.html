Trust System Explained

Why Casino Trust Scores Matter

When you're tilted, you're vulnerable. Scam casinos know this and target frustrated players with promises of "guaranteed wins" and "instant deposits."

TiltCheck's trust system protects you by flagging sketchy sites before you deposit.

How Trust Scores Work

Each casino gets a trust score from 0 (total scam) to 100 (highly trusted).

Scores are calculated using multiple data sources:

1. Domain Age & Registration

  • Brand new domains (< 6 months) get penalized
  • Privacy-protected WHOIS records raise red flags
  • Domains registered in known scam-heavy regions get scrutinized

2. Community Reports

  • User-submitted scam accusations (verified for legitimacy)
  • Withdrawal denial complaints
  • Bonus abuse accusations (casino blocking payouts over "violations")

3. License Verification

  • Does the casino claim a Cura√ßao/Malta/UK license?
  • Can we verify that license with the issuing authority?
  • Has the license been revoked or suspended?

4. Payment History

  • Average withdrawal time (instant vs. 30+ days)
  • Percentage of withdrawals completed successfully
  • Reports of "account locked, funds seized" situations

5. Social Proof

  • Reddit mentions (positive vs. negative sentiment)
  • Trustpilot reviews (filtered for fake reviews)
  • Discord/Telegram community activity (bots vs. real users)

Can Trust Scores Change?

Yes. Trust scores are updated regularly based on new data:

  • A casino with a good score can drop if withdrawal complaints spike
  • A new casino can improve its score over time with positive payment history
  • License revocations trigger immediate score drops
